## Here is a basic idea of my *little* project.

Right now, I'm using `GitHub` to get informations, software and learn things about `IT` and `cybersecurity`. I like to fork some repos that I will need to get again in the future or some others that I just want to add to my *To-Read-Later* collection. 

In the past year, I spend most of my free time using `HackTheBox Academy` and `TryHackMe`. I have keeped tracks and documented nearly everything I have learned and practiced in the cybersecurity field. The result is an `Obsidian` vault containing 1500+ notes in `Markdown` format.  

I've also gained some interest in static site generators and `GitPages`. I would like to create a nice website where students could find complementary informations to help them get a deeper comprehension and a better mastery of the skills teached on `TryHackMe` and `HackTheBox Academy`. I think there is a more constructive way to help people than just giving them another straigt-to-the-answer writeup. 
Don't get me wrong, I find them usefuls when I am getting stuck by a nasty question or when I don't fully understand how to use a new tool. But I often find them frustrating because they don't explain enough the methodology and the tought process behind the actions needed to solve a challenging room/course and I don't get the satisfaction from solving a challenge.  On the contrary, I often feel like I have cheated.
